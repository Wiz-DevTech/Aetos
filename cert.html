Certification FAQs

Answers to common questions about our certification programs

How long does certification take?

Our standard FAA Part 107 certification program typically takes 2-4 weeks, depending on your schedule and prior experience. More advanced certifications may take 4-6 weeks. We offer both full-time intensive courses and part-time options for working professionals.

What are the prerequisites for certification?

For FAA Part 107 certification, you must be at least 16 years old and able to read, speak, write, and understand English. No prior drone experience is required. For our advanced programs, we recommend either prior certification or significant flight experience.

Do you offer exam retakes if I don't pass?

Yes, all our certification programs include one free retake of the final examination. Additional preparation materials and instructor support are also provided between attempts at no extra cost.

Is financial aid or military discount available?

We offer a 15% discount for active military, veterans, and first responders. Payment plans are available for all programs, and we partner with several organizations that provide scholarships for qualifying candidates.

How long is certification valid?

FAA Part 107 certification is valid for 24 months. Our advanced and specialized certifications typically require renewal every 12-18 months with continuing education credits, which we help facilitate through our alumni program.
